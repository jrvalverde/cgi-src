WebGramm Help

This web form allows you to perform a ligand-receptor molecular docking (prediction of the structure of a complex of two molecules) using GRAMM at high or low resolution. No information about the binding site is needed, GRAMM will perform an exhaustive 6-dimensional search through the relative translations and rotations of the molecules using an empirical approach.

GRAMM allows docking prediction at high resolution (more expensive in computer power but more accurate) and low resolution (faster but less acccurate, though it may be used with lowly resolved molecules). It may work with two proteins, a pair ot transmembrane helices or ligand-receptor complexes. This form allows you to perform a high or low resolution docking of ligand-receptor complexes with fixed parameters.

To work with this form you must supply the structure of the two molecules in PDB format. If you have the structures in any other format, you may try BABEL for conversion between your format and PDB.

With the WebGramm form, you can supply different values to run GRAMM;
these values are reflected in the rpar.gr file ( you may have a look at the file
when the job completes ).

  • Matching mode: generic or helix.
  • High/low resolution docking: GRAMM uses the projection of an atom as a sphere with the van der Waals radius ( high ) or the grid-step radius ( low ).
  • Docking type resolution: all or hydrophobic.

After you have supplied the two molecules and submitted the form, GRAMM will be run with your set of parameters that work reasonably well for a high or low resolution docking of ligand-receptor complexes. You may have a look at the parameters used when the job completes.

Once your job is finished you will get a results page showing:

  • The molecules you supplied (ligand and receptor)
  • The parameters used to run GRAMM
  • The results summary, with a listing of the best 1000 matches and their computed energy
  • A navigator of the structures of the matches (with various visualization options) in batches of 10

NOTE: to get access to the full capabilities of GRAMM it is better to use it directly on the command line. If you are not a registered EMBnet/CNB user, you may get GRAMM for your platform from the author (see link above).
