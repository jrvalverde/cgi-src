RBDB

RBGMdb is an updated (as of 2005) Retinoblastoma Gene Mutation Database. A manuscript describing it in more detail is in preparation. The data collection is the result of work by Dr. A. Pestaña and his group. The RDBMS implementation, search methods and update procedures are the work of Dr. José R. Valverde.

A short description of the database contents follows:

Location:
Exon (E) and intron (I) number according to cDNA sequence NCBI (NM_000321.1)
Genomic:
Description follows the recommendations published by Donnan and Antonarakis (2000) using the genomic sequence GenBank: L11910.1
cDNA:
changes as in Donnen and Antonarakis (2000), using the cDNA sequence NCBI: NM_000321.1.
Protein:
Deduced changes at the protein level follow the recommendatios of Dunnen and Antonarakis (2000) usin the protein sequence NCBI: NP_000312.1.
Consequences:
predicted consequences are as follows:
  • regulation (promoter)
  • FS (trunckating frameshift)
  • IF (non-truncating inframe changes)
  • MS (missense changes)
  • NS (non-sense trunckating mutations)
  • SP (trunckating mutations affecting splicing sites)
  • SP-IF (in frame exon deletion due to splicing mutations)
  • SP-MS (mutations affecting the last two nucleotides in exon can either be considered as MS or splincing mutations.
Type of mutation:
  • DUP (duplication)
  • IN (insertion)
  • DE (deletion)
  • I_D (complex insertion and deletion)
  • PM (point mutation).
Origin:
Germline or somatic.
Sample:
  • PB (peripheral blood for germline)
  • retino (retinoblastoma)
  • other (other tumors).
Phenotype:
  • B (sporadic bilateral)
  • BF (bilateral familiar)
  • U (sporadic unilateral)
  • UF (unilateral familiar)
  • UMF (unilateral multifocal)
  • LP (familiar with low penetrance).
Sex:
  • F (female)
  • M (male)
Age (months):
Age at diagnosis or treatment in months.
Country:
Country of origin of probands or of the main research group in publications.
References
linked to PubMed.
Patient_ID:
as reported in publication.
Remarks:
any observation which can be useful in the context of a given mutation
