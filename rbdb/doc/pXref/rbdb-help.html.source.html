  27  A manuscript describing it in more detail is in preparation. The data
  28  collection is the result of work by Dr. A. Pesta&ntilde;a and his group.
  29  The RDBMS implementation, search methods and update procedures are the
  30  work of Dr. Jos&eacute; R. Valverde.</blockquote>

  32  <TR><TD BGCOLOR="lightblue">
  33  <h3>A short description of the database contents follows:</h3>
  34  
  35  <dl>
  36  <dt><strong>Location:</strong>
  37      <dd>Exon (E) and intron (I) number according to cDNA sequence NCBI 
  38      (NM_000321.1)
  39  
  40  <dt><strong>Genomic:</strong>
  41      <dd>Description follows the recommendations published by Donnan and 
  42      Antonarakis (2000) using the genomic sequence GenBank: L11910.1
  43  
  44  <dt><strong>cDNA:</strong> 
  45      <dd>changes as in Donnen and Antonarakis (2000), using the cDNA 
  46      sequence NCBI: NM_000321.1.
  47  
  48  <dt><strong>Protein:</strong> 
  49      <dd>Deduced changes at the protein level follow the recommendatios of 
  50      Dunnen and Antonarakis (2000) usin the protein sequence NCBI: NP_000312.1.
  51  
  52  <dt><strong>Consequences:</strong> 
  53      <dt>predicted consequences are as follows: 
  54      <ul>
  55          <li><em>regulation</em> (promoter)</li>
  56      <li><em>FS</em> (trunckating frameshift)</li>
  57      <li><em>IF</em> (non-truncating inframe changes)</li>
  58      <li><em>MS</em> (missense changes)</li>
  59      <li><em>NS</em> (non-sense trunckating mutations)</li>
  60      <li><em>SP</em> (trunckating mutations affecting splicing sites)</li>
  61      <li><em>SP-IF</em> (in frame exon deletion due to splicing mutations)</li>
  62      <li><em>SP-MS</em> (mutations affecting the last two nucleotides in 
  63      exon can either be considered as  MS or splincing mutations.</li>
  64      </ul>
  65  
  66  <dt><strong>Type of mutation:</strong>
  67      <dd><ul>
  68          <li><em>DUP</em> (duplication)</li>
  69      <li><em>IN</em> (insertion)</li>
  70      <li><em>DE</em> (deletion)</li>
  71      <li><em>I_D</em> (complex insertion and deletion)</li>
  72      <li><em>PM</em> (point mutation).</li>
  73      </ul>
  74      
  75  <dt><strong>Origin:</strong> 
  76      <dd>Germline or somatic.
  77  
  78  <dt><strong>Sample:</strong>
  79      <dd><ul>
  80          <li><em>PB</em> (peripheral blood for germline)</li>
  81      <li><em>retino</em> (retinoblastoma)</li>
  82      <li><em>other</em> (other  tumors).</li>
  83      </ul>
  84  
  85  <dt><strong>Phenotype:</strong> 
  86      <dd><ul>
  87          <li><em>B</em> (sporadic bilateral)</li>
  88      <li><em>BF</em> (bilateral familiar)</li> 
  89      <li><em>U</em> (sporadic unilateral)</li>
  90      <li><em>UF</em> (unilateral familiar)</li>
  91      <li><em>UMF</em> (unilateral multifocal)</li>
  92      <li><em>LP</em> (familiar with low penetrance).</li>
  93      </ul>
  94  
  95  <dt><strong>Sex:</strong> 
  96      <dd><ul>
  97          <li><em>F</em> (female)</li>
  98      <li><em>M</em> (male)</li>
  99      </ul>
 100  
 101  <dt><strong>Age (months):</strong>
 102      <dd>Age at diagnosis or treatment in months.
 103  
 104  <dt><strong>Country:</strong>
 105      <dd>Country  of origin of probands or of the main research group in 
 106      publications.
 107  
 108  <dt><strong>References</strong>
 109      <dd>linked to PubMed.
 110  
 111  <dt><strong>Patient_ID:</strong> 
 112      <dd>as reported in publication.
 113  
 114  <dt><strong>Remarks:</strong> 
 115      <dd>any observation which can be useful in the context of a given mutation
 116  </dl>
