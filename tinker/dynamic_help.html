TINKER

Software Tools for Molecular Design

DYNAMIC

Performs a molecular dynamics (MD) or stochastic dynamics (SD) computation. Starts either from a specified input molecular structure or from a structure- velocity-acceleration set saved from a previous dynamics trajectory (a restart from a .dyn file). MD trajectories are propagated using either a modified Beeman or a velocity Verlet integration method. SD is implemented via our own derivation of a velocity Verlet-based algorithm. In addition the program can perform full crystal calculations, and can operate in constant energy mode or with maintenance of a desired temperature and/or pressure using the Groningen method of coupling to external baths.

The simulation may be pictured as taking your molecule and putting it in a bath at the desired temperature for a period of time. This kind of simulations may help you picture the dynamic behaviour of your molecule at a given temperature.

This page will first convert your structure to PDB format using BABEL, and then process that structure with TINKER, first converting it to Tinker's XYZ format, then running DYNAMIC and finally converting TINKER output back to PDB format.

Input: You must supply:
  • the coordinates of your molecule in any of BABEL supported formats, either as a local file selected from your computer or pasting the coordinate data in the supplied box.
  • the input format of your data (make sure it matches what you have or you may get entirely wrong results)
  • the force field to use in the minimization procedure
  • desired number of steps that the symulation is going to last
  • time length of each simulation step
  • target temperature (in degrees Kelvin) to use
  • an e-mail address where the results (see below) will be sent
  • an optional urgent-mail address (e.g. a WAP mobile phone) where a short notification may be sent (so you may know it's done ASAP)
  • a short title to identify your job (this is for your own reference purposes, so you can identify the results when they are mailed to you)

Output will be sent to you by e-mail at the specified address. It will consist of several files:

  • [LOG] the simulation statistics
  • [XYZ] the modelled structure in TINKER XYZ format
  • [PDB] the modelled structure in PDB format
  • [SEQ] the sequence corresponding to your structure
  • [DYN] a dynamic snapshot including atomic velocities and accelerations

You may also specify an additional e-mail address to receive a short notification. Typically this would be a mobile phone e-mail address. This way, if your results are important to you, you will be notified of program termination as soon as possible.

Please, note that we don't send you the full simulation snapshots and trajectory, only the latest conformation saved. Trajectory files easily become HUGE (in the order of several megabytes) and often give trouble with e-mail. If you want to analyze trajectory files, please use the command line.
