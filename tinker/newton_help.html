NEWTON

A truncated Newton minimization method which requires potential energy, gradient and Hessian information. This procedure has significant advantages over standard Newton methods, and is able to minimize very large structures completely. Several options are provided with respect to minimization method and preconditioning of the Newton equations. The default options are recommended unless the user is familiar with the math involved. This program operates in Cartesian coordinate space and is fairly tolerant of poor input structures. Typical algorithm iteration times are longer than with nonlinear conjugate gradient or variable metric methods, but many fewer iterations are required for complete minimization. NEWTON is usually the best choice for minimizations to the 0.01 to 0.000001 kcal/mole/Å level of rms gradient convergence. Tests for directions of negative curvature can be removed, allowing NEWTON to be used for optimization to conformational transition state structures (this only works if the starting point is very close to the transition state).

Typically you would use another method that advances and converges faster to start the minimization (like MINIMIZE or OPTIMIZE) and refine your structure to a coarser RMS in order to get closer to the target state faster. Then you would use the structure you obtained with this method to fine tune it down to a highly accurate RMS.

This page will first convert your structure to PDB format using BABEL, and then process that structure with TINKER, first converting it to Tinker's XYZ format, then running MINIMIZE and finally converting TINKER output back to PDB format.

Input: You must supply:
  • the coordinates of your molecule in any of BABEL supported formats, either as a local file selected from your computer or pasting the coordinate data in the supplied box.
  • the input format of your data (make sure it matches what you have or you may get entirely wrong results)
  • the force field to use in the minimization procedure
  • target RMS gradient value at which the minimization will terminate (in Kcal/mole/Angstrom)
  • Method: use Automatic unless you understand the math involved
  • Precondition: use Auto unless you understand the math involved
  • an e-mail address where the results (see below) will be sent
  • an optional urgent-mail address (e.g. a WAP mobile phone) where a short notification may be sent (so you may know it's done ASAP)
  • a short title to identify your job (this is for your own reference purposes, so you can identify the results when they are mailed to you)

Output will be sent to you by e-mail at the specified address. It will consist of several files:

  • [LOG] the minimization statistics
  • [XYZ] the minimized structure in TINKER XYZ format
  • [PDB] the minimized structure in PDB format
  • [SEQ] the sequence corresponding to your structure

You may also specify an additional e-mail address to receive a short notification. Typically this would be a mobile phone e-mail address. This way, if your results are important to you, you will be notified of program termination as soon as possible.
