Welcome!

I'm Moldy, the Protein Modelling Wizard. Me and my friends will guide you through the wonderful world of Molecular Modelling.

Introduction

In this Journey we will explore the Molecular Dynamics of Biological Macromolecules, concentrating on modelling protein structure.

Doing in silico experiments is much like doing wet lab experiments: look up the bibliography to see to see the protocols used by your colleagues, adapt them to your problem and off you go! They are unlike real experiments though, in that you don't get real life results, but rather theoretical approximations.

The basic protocol

Modelling proteins in silico is a highly demanding task that can't be accomplished from scratch. You must follow a number of steps to seek a successful outcome:

  • Selecting a protein to study
  • Getting to know the protein characteristics
  • Obtain an initial approximation to the structure
  • Minimize the energy using Molecular Mechanics
  • Look for alternate configurations using Molecular Dynamics
  • Analyze your protein

Further details

The protein modelling process is not a simple step-by-step protocol; it works more like clay modelling: we start with an approximate sketch and refine it proggressively by repeating the basic protocol until we are satisfied with the quality of our work.

Here be dragons

Modelling is not an exact science: computer modeling makes use of many shortcuts and approximations, therefore you should never trust blindly the results: you must always check the results to see if they make sense and are coherent with experimental data. Since each protein is different there are no other rules than common sense.
